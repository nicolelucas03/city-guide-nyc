Parks

Central Park
  • Address: New York, NY 10024
  • Hours: Daily, 6:00 AM - 1:00 AM
  • Details: A sprwling oasis with walking paths, lakes, and numerous attractions like the Central Park Zoo. Great spot for picnics, jogging, and relaxing.
Prospect Park
  • Address: Brooklyn, NY 11225
  • Hours: Daily, 5:00 AM - 1:00 AM
  • Details: Brooklyn's major park, featuring a lake, Brooklyn Botanic Garden, and various sports facilities. It's a bit less crowded than Central Park and offers beautiful green spaces.
High Line
  • Address: New York, NY 10011
  • Hours: Daily, 7:00 AM - 10:00 PM
  • Price Range: $30 (adults), $22 (seniors), $17 (students)
  • Details: An elevated linear park built on a former rail line, featuring gardens, art installations, and great views of the city. A unique and scenic way to explore Manhattan's West Side.

Museums

The Metropolitan Museum of Art (The Met)
  • Address: 1000 Fifth Ave, New York, NY 10028
  • Hours: Sunday to Thursday, 10:00 AM - 5:30 PM; Friday and Saturday, 10:00 AM - 9:00 PM
  • Details: One of the world's largest and most comprehensive art museums. The Met's collection spans over 5,000 years of art from around the globe.
American Museum of Natural History
  • Address: Central Park West & 79th St, New York, NY 10024
  • Hours: Daily, 10:00 AM - 5:30 PM
  • Price Range: $23 (adults), $18 (seniors and students), $13 (children)
  • Details: Known for its impressive dinosaur fossils, the Hall of Ocean Life, and the Hayden Planetarium. Great for families and science enthusiasts.
Museum of Modern Art (MoMA)
  • Address: 11 W 53rd St, New York, NY 10019
  • Hours: Sunday to Monday, 10:30 AM - 5:30 PM; Friday, 10:30 AM - 8:00 PM
  • Price Range: $25 (adults), $18 (seniors), $14 (students), free for children 16 and under
  • Details: Home to an extensive collection of modern and contemporary art, including works by Picasso, Van Gogh, and Warhol.

Activities

Broadway Shows
  • Location: Theater District, New York, NY
  • Details: Catching a Broadway show is a quintessential New York experience. From long-running classics to new productions, there's always something exciting to see. Book tickets in advance for the best seats and prices.
Top of the Rock Observation Deck
  • Address: 30 Rockefeller Plaza, New York, NY 10112
  • Hours: Daily, 8:00 AM - 12:00 AM
  • Price Range: $40 (adults), $38 (seniors), $34 (children)
  • Details: Offers stunning views of the city skyline, including Central Park and the Empire State Building. The best time to visit is either early morning or late afternoon for the best light.
Statue of Liberty and Ellis Island
  • Location: Liberty Island and Ellis Island, New York, NY
  • Hours: Daily, 8:30 AM - 4:00 PM (varies seasonally)
  • Price Range: $24.50 (adults), $21 (seniors), $12 (children)
  • Details: Take a ferry to visit these historic landmarks. The Statue of Liberty symbolizes freedom and democracy, while Ellis Island offers a deep dive into immigrant history.

Fun Fact

A fun fact about New York is that it is home to the world’s first-ever underground park, called the Lowline. While New York City is famous for its iconic parks like Central Park and the High Line, the Lowline project is especially unique because it aims to transform an abandoned underground trolley terminal into a lush green park, entirely below street level. Using innovative solar technology to bring sunlight underground, the Lowline project showcases New York’s creativity in reclaiming and reimagining urban spaces. It’s still a developing project, but when completed, it will offer visitors a chance to enjoy nature in an underground setting!

Hidden Treasures

A hidden treasure of New York is The Elevated Acre, a secluded park tucked away in the Financial District. Despite being in one of the busiest areas of the city, this one-acre green oasis is often overlooked by tourists and even locals. Located at 55 Water Street, the Elevated Acre is accessible via an escalator tucked between towering office buildings. Once you reach the top, you're greeted with a beautiful lawn, lush gardens, wooden benches, and stunning views of the East River and Brooklyn. It's a perfect spot for a quiet lunch break or a peaceful escape from the bustling streets below. This hidden gem offers tranquility in the heart of New York's financial hub.
